Abstract

Recent advancements in text-to-image (T2I) generation have produced visually stunning results, yet models often struggle with complex prompts that require compositional reasoning. This limitation stems from a lack of large-scale, high-quality datasets specifically designed to train and evaluate such capabilities. To address this gap, we introduce FLUX-Reason-6M, a new dataset with over 6 million text-image pairs meticulously curated to cover diverse reasoning tasks, including spatial relationships, counting, attribute binding, and complex interactions. Furthermore, we present PRISM-Bench, a comprehensive and challenging benchmark derived from our dataset to rigorously assess the reasoning abilities of T2I models. Our benchmark provides a multi-faceted evaluation, offering deeper insights into model strengths and weaknesses beyond simple prompt-image similarity. We demonstrate the utility of FLUX-Reason-6M by fine-tuning several state-of-the-art models, showing significant improvements in their reasoning performance on PRISM-Bench. Our contributions provide the community with a valuable resource for building and evaluating the next generation of T2I models with advanced reasoning skills.

Contributions

  • We introduce FLUX-Reason-6M, the largest-to-date text-to-image dataset specifically designed for compositional reasoning, containing over 6 million carefully curated pairs.
  • We propose PRISM-Bench, a comprehensive benchmark with fine-grained categories to provide a holistic and challenging evaluation of the reasoning capabilities of T2I models.
  • We provide an in-depth analysis of current state-of-the-art T2I models using PRISM-Bench, revealing key areas for improvement and demonstrating the effectiveness of fine-tuning on FLUX-Reason-6M.
